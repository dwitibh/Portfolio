NPS Intelligence Platform & Action Planning

An end-to-end analytics platform that transformed Net Promoter Score survey data into operational intelligence across 411 accounts, combining Power BI reporting with a GPT-4o summarisation engine on Microsoft Fabric to surface actionable feedback at scale, driving the business to an NPS of 63 in the Excellent B2B category.

The Problem

The business was collecting thousands of NPS survey responses per year across multiple service lines and divisions: Technical Services, Business Services, Communities, and Projects. The data sat in siloed spreadsheets. Operational managers had no way to connect a customer's score to which site, contract, or team was responsible, and no scalable way to act on the verbatim feedback buried in open-text comments.

The core questions were: Which contracts are at risk? Which divisions drive the most detractors? And what are customers actually saying, at scale, contract by contract?

Approach

1. Data Pipeline & Integration

Survey responses were ingested from the survey platform and landed in a Microsoft Fabric Lakehouse. PySpark orchestrated the data processing, joining each survey response to the operational data model, mapping each record to an account, division, SAM, managing director, and service line.

2. Row Level Security

RLS was applied at two levels. At the data layer, an Azure Data group structure was used to restrict access so that sensitive account content could not be viewed across the organisation. Only users assigned to a given account group could access the underlying survey data, action records, and verbatim comments for that account. This ensured that commercially sensitive client feedback never surfaced in the wrong hands.

In the Action Planning module, a second layer of account-level RLS was applied directly in Power BI. Each account manager could only see the action plan status, progress, and client feedback summary for their own accounts. A managing director or regional director could see across their portfolio, but an individual SAM had no visibility into peers' accounts. This hierarchy was defined once in the semantic model and automatically applied across every page of the dashboard.

3. GPT-4o Verbatim Summarisation

Open-text comments from 1,109 survey responses were processed through a GPT-4o summarisation pipeline built on Microsoft Fabric. Rather than reading comments manually, the pipeline grouped comments by contract using PySpark, then called the Azure OpenAI API (GPT-4o) with carefully engineered system and user prompts.

The system prompt framed the model as a business analyst tasked with extracting plain-text insights. The user prompt constrained output to no more than 5 bullet points per contract, covering key themes, common issues, positive mentions, and actionable trends. Temperature was set to 0 to ensure deterministic, consistent output across all contracts. The summarised results were written back to the Lakehouse as a table and surfaced directly in Power BI, meaning account managers could read a concise AI summary per client without any data science intermediation.

4. Action Planning Dashboard

Beyond the NPS score itself, the platform included a full Action Planning module, tracking 877 action plans across 279 accounts (68% of the portfolio). Each action was tagged with a key theme, owner, status, and target completion date, giving leadership real-time visibility into what was being done about the feedback, not just what the feedback was.

What I Learned

Getting the data model right before building the dashboard saved weeks of rework. The Row Level Security structure, once embedded in the semantic model, made it trivial to expand to new divisions and managing directors, which proved critical as the scope grew mid-project.

On the GPT side, engineering the prompts carefully was the real unlock. Setting temperature to 0 ensured every contract summary followed a consistent format, something that would have been impossible with higher randomness settings. Keeping the system prompt focused on a business analyst persona, and constraining the user prompt to five or fewer plain-text bullets, meant the output landed directly in Power BI without any post-processing. The account managers didn't need to know how it worked; they just had a useful summary per client.

The Action Planning module was the part that required the most stakeholder alignment. Capturing action owner, theme, status, and date in a structured format rather than free text was what made the dashboard genuinely useful rather than just a reporting view.
