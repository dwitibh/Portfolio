Key Insights

Six findings that drove the action plan

01 · Division Gap

Technical Services is dragging the whole portfolio by an estimated 11 NPS points

Business Services sits at NPS 80, Communities at 71. Technical Services sits at 11, 52 points below the next division and well below the FM sector average. If it matched the cross-division average, overall NPS would reach ~74.

TS detractor rate: 38% vs 4–10% across other divisions
02 · Detractor Concentration

14 accounts generate 58% of all negative responses, this is a targeted problem, not a portfolio-wide one

Every one of these 14 accounts sits in Technical Services or Projects. All share a common pattern: no completed action plan in the prior 12-month cycle. The GPT-4o summaries flagged recurring phrases: "same issues raised last year" and "promised but not delivered."

Resolving these 14 accounts adds ~4 NPS points
03 · Passive Opportunity

Passives are the single largest untapped growth lever, converting half adds ~7 NPS points

22% of respondents scored 7 or 8. GPT-4o verbatim analysis showed these clients are not at churn risk, they're satisfied but not advocating. The dominant theme was "good service, poor communication," cited in 41% of passive comments.

Converting 50% of passives to promoters → NPS ~70
04 · Action Plan Impact

Accounts with 3+ completed actions score 33 points higher on average, but 32% of at-risk accounts have no plan

The data shows a clear link between structured follow-up and client sentiment. Accounts with 3+ completed actions averaged NPS 61. Accounts with no plan at all averaged 28. The 32% gap is the next phase of the programme.

9-point average lift per action plan cycle completed
05 · Verbatim at Scale

1,109 open-text responses summarised automatically, account managers read a 5-bullet AI brief per client, not raw feedback

Before the platform, verbatim feedback sat unread in spreadsheets. The GPT-4o pipeline grouped responses by contract, extracted themes, and wrote each summary in under 2 seconds. Zero manual reading. Zero data science intermediation.

Top customer words: Collaborative · Professional · Reliable
06 · 7-Year Trajectory

From detractor territory in 2018 to sector-leading in 2025, but the biggest lever for growth remains untouched

The business moved from NPS -10 in 2018 to 63 in 2025, a 73-point improvement. NPS 63 sits 25 points above the FM sector average of ~38. The ceiling is ~74 if Technical Services reaches division average, a reachable target within two survey cycles.

Recommendations

Three teams, three clear actions

Technical Services Leadership

Fix the service delivery loop before the next survey cycle

  • Auto-flag accounts with PPM completion below 85% for SAM alert in dashboard
  • Mandate client communication within 24 hours of all planned maintenance works
  • Set division target: Technical Services NPS to 40+ by end of year
Account Management · SAM Team

Convert passives through a structured relationship cadence

  • All passive accounts (7–8) to receive proactive review within 60 days of survey close
  • 14 high-detractor accounts to receive named executive sponsor and 30-day outreach plan
  • Minimum 2 formal account reviews per year enforced and tracked in the dashboard
Managing Directors

Close the action plan coverage gap before NPS plateaus

  • 4 MD regions below 50% action plan coverage, escalate to ensure named owners by Q3
  • Target: reduce accounts with no active plan from 32% to under 10%
  • Report action plan completion rate as a board KPI alongside the NPS score itself
